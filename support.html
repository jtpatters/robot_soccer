About the app

Robot Soccer 3D is a friendly programming puzzle for iPad. You build a short program out of blocks — walk, turn, kick, wait, loop, and so on — then press play and watch your robot try to score the goal. It's offline, ad-free, sign-in-free, and the whole game lives on your device.

Frequently asked questions

What iPad do I need?

The app runs on any iPad with iPadOS 16.4 or later. It is designed for landscape and full-screen play. Older iPads stuck on iPadOS 15 or earlier are not supported.

How do I get started?

From the title screen, tap How to Play. The interactive tutorial walks you through finding your player, picking them by jersey number, adding a kick block, and pressing play. After that the game introduces a new mechanic every couple of levels.

I can't see the player I'm supposed to pick.

Drag anywhere on the field with one finger to spin the camera around. The blue robot standing right next to the ball is the one you'll command — look for the gold arrow over their head during the tutorial.

The buttons feel too small / animations feel too fast.

Tap into the dev panel (touch and hold the top-right corner of the screen for about a second) and you'll find sliders for Button size and Speed. Settings are saved between sessions.

How do I reset my star progress?

Open the dev panel (long-press the top-right corner) and tap Reset all stars. This clears every level's best star count immediately. It cannot be undone.

I beat level 12 but didn't get all the stars. Can I keep trying?

Yes! Stars track your best performance per level. Replay any level any time — your best star count only goes up, never down.

Does the app collect any data about me or my child?

No. The app is fully offline once installed. It does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information, and it does not include accounts, ads, in-app purchases, or third-party tracking. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Reporting a bug

If something looks wrong or doesn't work the way you expect, please send an email with as much of the following as you can gather:

  • Which iPad model you're using (Settings → General → About → Model Name)
  • Your iPadOS version (Settings → General → About → iPadOS Version)
  • The level you were on
  • What you tapped just before the problem
  • A screenshot or short screen recording, if it's visual
